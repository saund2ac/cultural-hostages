Leading human rights lawyer Geoffrey Robertson QC accuses the British Museum of exhibiting "pilfered cultural property" and calls for the return of treasures taken from "subjugated peoples" by conquerors or colonial masters. Robertson criticizes the museum for allowing an unofficial "stolen goods tour," which includes items like the Elgin Marbles, Hoa Hakananaiâ€™a, and the Benin bronzes, all of which are wanted by Greece, Easter Island, and Nigeria, respectively. He suggests that the museum should return Elgin's loot and advocates for the return of cultural property based on human rights law principles. The British Museum defends its acquisitions, stating that the Elgin marbles were obtained legally with the approval of Ottoman authorities and that it acknowledges the difficult histories of some collections.
